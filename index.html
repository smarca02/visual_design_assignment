mockups

INTRODUCTION: the original idea for this web project sprang from a small real-life issue. I own a prolific Aloe Vera plant so, as new pots have piled up in my house, on occasions, I found myself offering a “baby plant” to friends and acquaintances.

OBJECTIVE: to design the website mockups for a fictional non-profit organisation composed by people with a shared passion for plants and gardening. The main group’s activity is to organise events where like-minded people can meet up to swap indoors and outdoors plants, propagations, cuttings, starts, seeds and other garden related items. I researched online in order to better understand how to develop the project’s plan and identify the group’s requirements in addition to users’ needs for the web site. I found at least one group that presents similar objectives and needs in the United Kingdom. Besides, “plant-swap” events have appeared to be popular not only in European cities such as London but also in the United States and have seen the participations of people from different age groups and backgrounds.

To follow up, I composed a moodboard with images collected from different free image sites (unsplash, pexels, pixabay and freepik). Although not required for this assignment, I found the moodboard to be an instrumental tool for identifying the colour palette as well as the choice of typography and images to include in the mockups.

I have produced these mockups in Adobe XD. The Homepage is presented both for web (full width 1280px) and mobile screen (full width 375px). The remaining pages have all been designed for mobile to conform to the "mobile first" approach. I adopted the help of layout grids during the design development phase. For the web homepage I used a 12-column grid, with a column width of 69 pixels, a gutter width of 16 pixels and 138-pixel margin per side. The mobile pages are based on a 4-column layout grid with 68-pixel column width, 10-pixel gutter and 36-pixel margin on both sides.
