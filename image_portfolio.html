The main purpose of my web project is to inform about “Bring a Plant” events and provide useful tips on how to take care of plants. The shop is an additional feature to help sustain the cost of running the activities. The group’s welcoming and inclusive policy is reflected in the choice of images showing happy people of different ages and backgrounds.

Image 1: Homepage image hero

“Bringing people and plants together” is the ethos of the “Bring a Plant” group, made immediately clear by the homepage’s image hero with the close-up of two hands holding a potted plant. The original image most likely had already gone through a degree of manipulation considering the blurriness surrounding the areas around the plant but I wanted it to have a narrower cut to give more importance to the hands-plant element. Additionally I aimed for the plant pot to be more defined and the plant’s foliage more vibrant. Besides, I planned to have the word “welcome”, in white, superimposed on the photo in correspondence with the hands, so I proceeded to darken the image areas in close proximity to the word for increased contrast making the text more readable.

PROCESS: I manipulated this image in Photoshop. After opening the original image, I duplicated it on a new layer and created a clipping path of the potted plant with the pen tool then deleted all the layer content but the plant through inverse selection and adjusted its vibrance and exposure. Then, I further blurred the background layer with curves making sure the human body parts would still be identifiable and the pot's vibrant colours would pop even more. I grouped both background and pot layer together. In order to give the image its final “leaf shape” I copied the image hero element from the homepage mockup and pasted it as a smart object into a new layer on Photoshop for reference only. I selected the transparent areas around the smart object through the magic wand tool, inverted the selection to obtain the leaf shape and created a mask on the image group. Finally, I added the text with the type tool, positioned it then converted it into raster to create a soft diffused shadow behind it.

SAVING AND OPTIMISING FOR THE WEB: if I had to preserve the image transparency I would have had the option of saving it either in PNG or GIF. However, considering the image is displayed on white background, I compromised on the alpha channel (that provides transparency) and saved it as JPG, very high, 80% quality, converted in sRGB, in order to have a remarkably reduced size compared to its GIF format equivalent. As the original image presents limited range of colours and blurred effect the loss of quality typical of JPEG formats is barely noticeable.

Image 2: Photo manipulation + colour correction

Whilst researching on suitable images I was not able to find any representing a group of people and plants together, so I attempted to recreate my own combining two different images. I intended to use this picture in the blog section but despite liking the enhanced result, I think the final image might look too polished for the purpose of the website.

PROCESS: on Photoshop I created a detailed clipping path of the plant pot with the pen tool on the original image that allowed me to select, cut and insert the plant element into the main picture with the two women sat at a table with coffee cups. I resized the plant image into a size that would proportionally work with the other elements and proceeded to adjustthe colours, th lighting and blur the pot's outline to closely match those in the original image. With the brush tool I painted the shadow at the bottom of the pot, then added a layer to adjust hue and saturation to blend the result event more. I added a new layer with a very subtle sepia to transparent gradient to further uniform the image elements and give it a “sunset” feeling. Finally, I cropped the image to its current size excluding part of the background so the focus would be on the main elements.

SAVING AND OPTIMISING FOR THE WEB: I saved this image in JPEG, very-high quality at 80% with no blur to preserve the deep range of colours with minimal quality loss.

Image 3: Colour correction

This photo of a cat moving between plant pots is showed in the mockups as the image for a blog article about pet-friendly plants. The original picture had a cold tinge and I wanted to enhance it with brighter colours and more contrast on both the cat’s red fur and the green plants and their coloured pots.

PROCESS: In Photoshop I used a variety of tools available in the image adjustments menu: I reduced the cold tone of the image modifying the values of the RGB channels with the channel mixer, increased the exposure, intervened on the image levels, added more contrast, adjusted the colour balance and improved the image vibrance. Lastly, I reduced the image size.

SAVING AND OPTIMISING FOR THE WEB: I saved the final image in JPEG format, converted in sRGB, very-high quality at 80%, which seemed a good compromise between size and colour richeness accounting for a small loss in quality.

Image 4: Vector image superimposed on photo

I produced a small selection of branded merchandising mockups to go in the shop web page. The gardener apron in this photo was one of them. I designed the logo from scratch in Adobe Illustrator.

PROCESS: I opened the AI vector file in Photoshop and pasted it as a smart object layer into the main picture of the woman wearing a plain blue apron. I scaled down the white logo to proportionally fit in the desired position corresponding to the top part of the apron. Then, I edited it with free transform and distorted the “B” letter to make it look like it was printed directly on the apron. I cropped the image to give it a portrait orientation and have the model wearing the apron as the predominant feature.

SAVING AND OPTIMISING FOR THE WEB: I saved the final image in JPEG high quality 60%, converted in sRGB, to preserve the richness of tones and colours reducing the image size substantially with minimal deterioration.

Image 5: Photo collage with colour manipulation

For the homepage I was looking for a striking image that would capture the essence of the group’s activities and their friendly and welcoming attitude. My solution was to combine monochromatic images of people and plants into one single picture with the common green hue that tied them all to symbolically represent the core elements of the group. More in detail, in the web page the image would be composed by a collection of 15 green monochrome sprites with each one revealing its coloured version when hovering over it with a mouse. In this instance I have embedded the complete collage image.

PROCESS: On Photoshop I set up a custom guide layout of 5 columns and 3 rows. I determined the size of each image tile (201x213 pixels) and the criteria for the composition by alternating plant only pictures and images of people’s face or hands. I proceeded to work on every single image converting the background to layer zero (with added transparency) in order to reduce the canvas in size, then resized the image dimensions to fit nicely in the canvas. Consequently, the image mode was changed from RGB to greyscale, its contrast values were adjusted to have higher variance and the final green hue applied through the selection of the medium and a light shade of green taken from the mockups colour palette. All tile images where imported into the composition file on separate layers and aligned using the grid as a guide.

SAVING AND OPTIMISING FOR THE WEB: the image was saved in JPEG format, very high, 80% quality with sRG conversion. The image deterioration is minimal and does not greatly affect the colors, high contrast and the small size of the tile images.

Image 6:

This is a vector file entirely drawn on Adobe Illustrator. My goal was to design a simple playful logo that represented the group’s identity through the combination of typography and illustrations with similar styles. The vector logo could be reproduced on a number of printed and digital media and effectively resized at any size without any quality deterioration.

PROCESS: after a few trials playing with different fonts I chose “Carley Co”, a skinny all caps font I had already installed in my laptop’s font folder. I amended the letter spacing and, on a separate layer, I drew the plant pot and leaves illustration with the pen tool and the circle tool adjusting the path’s angle and curve points with the Bezier handles. Then I increased the stroke size to match the letters thickness, expanded the plant pot and 2 leaves paths and merged then into one single vector element so the stroke of the illustration could be proportionally rescaled and finally I expanded the appearance of the font transforming it into a path, grouped both letters and illustration into one vector on a single layer and changed its colour to white.

SAVING AND OPTIMISING FOR THE WEB: the vector illustration was exported for the web directly in Adobe Illustrator and saved in PNG format using the illustrator default settings to maintain a neat and image with crisp edges on a transparent layer that would make it suitable to be superimposed on other contrasting coloured elements such as green background on the final image.
